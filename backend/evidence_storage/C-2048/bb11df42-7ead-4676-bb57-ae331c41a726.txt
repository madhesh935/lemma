SECTION 2 — CIRCUMSTANCES OF DEATH
============================================================================

The deceased was found at approximately 22:30 hrs on 26 April 2025 near
Platform 4, E-Gate 4, Chennai Central Railway Station. The body was
discovered by railway security personnel (RPF Constable M. Selvam) lying
face-down in a poorly illuminated area adjacent to a loading bay.

First responders noted significant blood pooling beneath the head and
abdominal region. No identification documents were found on the body at
the time of discovery. The body was later identified by the spouse at
GH Mortuary.

Police FIR registered under Sec. 302 IPC (Murder) by Inspector K. Rajan,
E-4 Flower Bazaar Police Station. Investigation Officer: SI Pradeep Kumar.

Body received at CFSL at 23:15 hrs. Post-mortem commenced at 23:47 hrs.

SECTION 7 — INJURY ANALYSIS & WEAPON CORRELATION
============================================================================

Weapon recovered: Iron rod, 87 cm length, ~2.5 cm diameter, weight 600g
Recovery location: 100 meters northeast of crime scene, in drainage canal
Condition: Blood staining on distal 15 cm, partial fingerprint

Pattern Analysis:
  The three patterned impact injuries on the occipital skull are consistent
  with the recovered iron rod. Diameter (2.5 cm) matches the linear
  depression pattern. Force estimation: high-energy impact (>150 newtons
  per strike). Trajectory analysis indicates right-handed assailant
  striking from behind and slightly right of the victim.

DNA Recovery:
  - Blood on iron rod: DNA Sample B-09
    → STR profile matches R. Suresh (victim) at 15/15 loci
  - Partial fingerprint on rod shaft
    → AFIS match: S-118 (Vetri) — partial, 74% confidence
  - Occipital wound blood (scalp sample): DNA Sample D-77
    → Mixed profile: major contributor = victim
    → Minor contributor: matches S-118 (Vetri) at 99.2% confidence

SECTION 8 — CAUSE OF DEATH
============================================================================

PRIMARY CAUSE OF DEATH:
  Blunt force trauma to the occipital region with depressed skull
  fracture and subarachnoid hemorrhage.

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS:
  1. Hepatic laceration with hemoperitoneum (~1,200 ml)
  2. Mesenteric tear with active internal hemorrhage

MANNER OF DEATH: HOMICIDE

MECHANISM OF DEATH:
  Craniocerebral injury → cerebral edema → brain stem compression
  Combined with hypovolemic shock secondary to abdominal hemorrhage

============================================================================
SECTION 9 — EVIDENCE OF POST-MORTEM MANIPULATION
============================================================================

  - Fixed livor mortis on anterior surface, but body discovered face-down
    in a position inconsistent with initial livor distribution
  - Post-mortem drag mark on left thigh (8.0 cm, no vital reaction)
  - Soil and debris pattern on posterior clothing inconsistent with
    discovery position
  - Blood trail analysis (CSI) indicates body moved approximately
    4 meters from initial position

CONCLUSION: Body was relocated post-mortem. Drag pattern and
environmental trace evidence suggest movement within 30–60 minutes
after death.

============================================================================
SECTION 10 — SUMMARY AND CONCLUSIONS
============================================================================

1. The deceased, R. Suresh (34M), died of blunt force trauma to the
   head caused by a cylindrical blunt weapon (iron rod). Three separate
   high-energy impacts to the occipital region caused depressed skull
   fracture, subarachnoid hemorrhage, and fatal cerebral edema.

2. Secondary fatal contribution from hepatic laceration (capsular tear
   6.5 cm) causing hemoperitoneum of ~1,200 ml, consistent with
   blunt abdominal impact.

3. Defensive bruise on left forearm confirms victim was conscious and
   attempted defense during the initial phase of the assault.

4. Trace diazepam (0.03 mg/L) detected — possible pre-assault
   involuntary sedation. Sub-therapeutic concentration suggests
   ingestion 4–6 hours before death.

5. Post-mortem body relocation confirmed by livor mortis distribution,
   drag marks, and environmental trace evidence.

6. DNA evidence (D-77) links suspect S-118 (Vetri) to the victim's
   occipital wound with 99.2% confidence.

7. Estimated time of death: 19:30–21:00 hrs, 26 April 2025.
